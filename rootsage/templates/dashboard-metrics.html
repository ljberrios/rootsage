Used in relatively large amounts by all living things, nitrogen (N) is an essential nutrient. It is a fundamental part of the chlorophyll molecule and is essential in the formation of amino acids and proteins. A good supply of N is associated with vigorous growth and deep green color. Plants deficient in N have stunted growth. Excess N may cause delayed initiation of flowering or fruiting, and susceptibility to plant diseases.

Required in lower amounts than other major nutrients, phosphorus (P) is critical in the early developmental stages of growth, and in energy transfer within the plant throughout the growing season. It is important for photosynthesis, respiration, cell division, and many other processes. Deficiency in P strongly affects overall growth rates and is often difficult to diagnose correctly from visual symptoms alone. These symptoms only become distinctive when other nutrients are well supplied.

Used in photosyntesis, cargohydrate transport, water regulation, and protein synthesis, potassium (K) is frequently associated with improved crop quality as well as better handling and storage properties. It is required by plants in approximately the same amounts as nitrogen. Potassium deficiency may cause the following symptoms:

  • Stunted growth
  • Development of poor root systems
  • Leaf shedding, which ultimately leads to low yields
